AI Incidents maintains a dated, sourced catalog of AI and ML system failures — adversarial misuse, model errors with real-world consequences, data breaches involving training sets, and deployment failures across sectors.

## What we catalog

- **Adversarial misuse** — documented cases where AI systems were manipulated or exploited to cause harm
- **Model failures** — production failures with measurable real-world impact, sourced and dated
- **Training-data and privacy incidents** — data breaches, unauthorized scraping, and training-set exposures
- **Regulatory enforcement** — fines, orders, and investigations tied to AI system failures
- **Post-mortems** — detailed failure analysis where documentation is sufficient

## Who reads this

Researchers, policy professionals, and security teams who need a reliable public record of how AI systems fail in the real world. Every entry cites primary sources and includes a structured timeline.

## Stay current

Subscribe to the RSS feed to track new incidents as they are catalogued. To submit a verified incident not yet in the database, contact the editorial desk with documentation and a primary-source link.
